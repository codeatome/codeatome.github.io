About Me

Hello! I'm Shwasa, a rising sophomore passionate about science, technology, leadership, and public impact. Growing up in Silicon Valley, I was surrounded by innovation—and I’ve always been curious about how things work. That curiosity soon developed into a strong interest in biology and computer science, especially in the ways they intersect to improve healthcare outcomes.

Whether it’s working on genomics research projects, mentoring younger students, or designing outreach programs for community nonprofits, I thrive in environments where I can combine analytical thinking with real-world problem-solving. I'm especially drawn to computational biology and health technology because of their potential to revolutionize the way we understand and treat disease.

Outside of academics, I enjoy debating, running, and learning new languages. I love diving into topics that blend logic and creativity, and I’m always looking for ways to grow—whether through collaboration, challenge, or change.
